Metrics Explained

Fuzzy Accuracy
Edit distance (insertions + deletions + substitutions) between OCR and truth, normalised by the longer string. A score of 90% means 10% of characters needed correction.
1 − edit_dist / max(len_ocr, len_truth)
Exact Match
Binary yes/no — does the normalised OCR string equal the normalised truth string exactly. The strictest possible measure. Spaces and punctuation are stripped before comparison.
normalize(ocr) == normalize(truth)
Precision
Of every character the OCR output, what fraction was actually in the truth. Low precision = hallucination (e.g. outputting 老人 or シーカーストーン when not in ground truth).
matched_chars / len(ocr)
Recall
Of every character in the truth, what fraction appeared in the OCR output. Low recall = dropped characters (e.g. ネ dropped from パネル, or small kana dropped).
matched_chars / len(truth)
F1 Score
Harmonic mean of precision and recall. Balances both failure modes. Preferred summary metric when both hallucination and dropping are relevant failure modes.
2 × P × R / (P + R)

Note on fuzzy vs F1: Fuzzy accuracy penalises length mismatches harshly — outputting two extra characters on a 20-char string hurts more than it should. F1 via character multiset intersection is more forgiving of positional errors but does not care about order. Both are shown because they expose different failure modes. Exact match is the 1:1 character score you asked for — a scene either passed or failed, no partial credit.

Every scene falls into one of two categories. They behave very differently — dialogue sits on a semi-transparent bar with furigana; item descriptions sit on a dark near-black background with no furigana.

The gap between dialogue and item description accuracy is enormous — and consistent across all variants. Dialogue scenes average 94%+ fuzzy across the board. Item description scenes average 65–80%. No variant achieved a single exact match on any item description scene. This is the single biggest quality gap in the system and is primarily driven by: (1) small kana degradation on dark backgrounds, (2) the item name region being concatenated into the output.

Key Findings & Recommendations

1 · Exact match rate is low across the board — but that is expected

The highest exact match rate is paddle_postprocess and paddle_merged at 38% (3/8 scenes). No variant reached exact match on any item description scene. This is partly because the item name from the secondary region is being concatenated into the output — if that were stripped, several more scenes would be exact matches. The じゃ→じや error alone blocks exact match on scene 02 for all Paddle variants.

Two fixes would dramatically improve exact match rate: (1) Strip the item name suffix before scoring — it's a region grouping artifact, not an OCR error. (2) Add "じや": "じゃ" to _EXACT_FIXES — this one substitution would fix scene 02 across all Paddle variants and push exact match to 50%+ for postprocess/merged.

2 · Apple Vision leads on fuzzy accuracy but has the worst precision

AV's recall is outstanding (97.9%) — it reads nearly every character that should be there. But its precision (91.2%) is the lowest of all variants because it consistently appends speaker labels ("老人") from a region adjacent to the dialogue crop. This is a bounds configuration issue. Fix the crop height and AV's precision likely reaches 97%+, making it the clear winner by all metrics.

3 · Paddle recall > Paddle precision on item descriptions

Across scenes 07–08, recall averages 80–85% but precision averages 72–76%. The Paddle variants are dropping characters (recall loss) but not hallucinating new ones (precision is still reasonable). The exception is furigana contamination on paddle_furigana where both drop.

4 · paddle_furigana's CC removal is the weakest furigana suppression strategy

4 out of 9 scenes contaminated vs 1 out of 8 for every other variant. The row-density approach used by postprocess/merged/reading_match is substantially more robust for this game's font. CC removal's theoretical precision advantage does not materialise in practice here.

5 · Recommended _EXACT_FIXES additions

Based on patterns appearing across multiple runs and scenes:

  • "じや": "じゃ" — systematic dakuten drop on scenes 02, 04 across all Paddle v5 variants
  • "チチ": "チュチュ" — consistent small ュ drop on dark background katakana (scenes 07, 09)
  • "そつく": "そっく" — っ→つ in そっくり, appears in 4 of 5 variants on scene 07
  • "パル": "パネル" — ネ drop in パネル, appears in 4 of 5 variants on scene 08

Note: verify each against full Japanese vocabulary before adding — some of these substrings may appear in unrelated words where the fix would be incorrect.

Metric definitions: All metrics computed after normalisation (spaces, 。、・…？！「」 stripped). Fuzzy accuracy: 1 − edit_distance(ocr, truth) / max(len_ocr, len_truth); Wagner-Fischer algorithm with substitution cost 1. Exact match: binary, normalize(ocr) == normalize(truth). Precision/Recall: character multiset intersection — counts how many characters (by type and frequency) the OCR and truth share, regardless of position. F1: harmonic mean of precision and recall. Small kana error: count of っッぁぃぅぇぉゃゅょァィゥェォャュョ in truth that are absent or incorrect in OCR output (multiset comparison). Furigana contamination: presence of known furigana reading patterns (たから, ねむ, たの, ひかほこら etc.) in OCR output. Duplicate scenes excluded from per-run averages. Item name suffixes (シーカーストーン, チュチュゼリー etc.) are included in OCR output and penalise precision scores — these are region grouping artifacts rather than OCR errors.
